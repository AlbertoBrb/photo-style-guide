01
Studio Transition & Setup
Operations Change management Set design
+

When NET-A-PORTER relocated to a new Milan facility under LuxExperience, I managed the full operational transition of the still life photography area — coordinating the physical move, designing and building all new shooting sets from scratch, and onboarding incoming freelance photographers, all while maintaining production continuity and keeping shoot targets on track.

Outcome

Full studio operational within schedule. New team productive from day one. Zero disruption to production output during transition.

02
Still Life Style Guide
Tool building Process design HTML / JS
+

Shot reference documentation for NAP and MRP existed in scattered PDFs and OneNote notebooks — inaccessible mid-shoot, inconsistent across photographers. I designed and built a web-based internal tool centralising all 338 shot references across both brands, with full-text search, brand switching, image preview, a producer edit mode, and a live JSON export. Actively used by the team daily.

Outcome

Single source of truth for the entire photo team. Reduced briefing time and shot errors. Immediate adoption, still in active daily use.

03
Madame — Global Tool Adoption
Change management Cross-team coordination Global rollout
+

As Madame Champion for the DC4 studio, I led the adoption of a new global workflow tool across the entire photography team — sustained communication with the product team, structured feedback collection, testing cycles for each update, and translating technical requirements into practical guidance for photographers and managers over multiple months.

Outcome

Full studio adoption. DC4 brought to global standard. Technical support described by management as "extremely important, especially when things get very technical."

04
Barcode Integration & Equipment Ops
Technology Workflow optimisation Equipment ownership
+

Identified a systemic bottleneck in the photographer workflow — manual product logging was creating overhead across all workstations. Proposed and implemented barcode readers integrated into the existing system. Beyond this, I hold ongoing responsibility for the full equipment lifecycle: inventory, maintenance, camera repair, procurement, and kit preparation for MPS, castings, and external shoots.

Outcome

Immediate reduction in overhead. Adopted across all workstations. Equipment operations described as "always perfect" — trusted without oversight.
